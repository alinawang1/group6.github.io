UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "About CHIS." *California Health Interview Survey*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/california-health-interview-survey-chis/about-chis. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This page introduces CHIS as the largest state health survey in the country, outlining its release formats (Public Use Files, AskCHIS, Health Profiles, and confidential data) and the range of topics it covers, from health care access to housing and climate change. We used it as the foundational source for describing what CHIS is and why it exists.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "Access CHIS Data." *California Health Interview Survey*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/california-health-interview-survey-chis/access-chis-data. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This page compares CHIS's different data access tools, including AskCHIS, AskCHIS Neighborhood Edition, and the Public Use Files, explaining who each tool is designed for. We used it to decide which data access method fit our project and to describe the dataset's structure in our data critique.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "Public Use Files." *California Health Interview Survey*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/public-use-files. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This page details the specific contents of the CHIS Public Use Files (PUFs), including the variables covered, available data formats, and the separate Adult, Teen, and Child files. We relied on it heavily to describe our dataset's structure and coverage in the data critique, and to explain what the PUFs can and cannot capture.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "CHIS Design and Methods." *California Health Interview Survey*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/california-health-interview-survey-chis/chis-design-and-methods. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This page explains CHIS's sampling methodology and survey design, including its status as a Charter Member of the AAPOR Transparency Initiative. We used it to describe how CHIS collects its data and to support our discussion of the survey's methodological strengths and limitations.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "Data Access Center." *California Health Interview Survey*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/data-access-center. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This page describes the Data Access Center (DAC), which provides secure access to confidential CHIS data not available in the Public Use Files, such as detailed geographic identifiers. We used it in our data critique to explain why fine-grained spatial analysis is not possible with the public data we are using.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "California Health Interview Survey (CHIS)." *UCLA Center for Health Policy Research*, healthpolicy.ucla.edu/our-work/california-health-interview-survey-chis. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This is the main landing page for CHIS, describing it as the nation's largest state health survey and summarizing who conducts and funds it. We used it to identify and describe the UCLA Center for Health Policy Research as the survey's home institution and to confirm its funding sources.

UCLA Center for Health Policy Research. "California Health Interview Survey (CHIS)." *UCLA Newsroom*, newsroom.ucla.edu/file?fid=61491baf2cfac27233c16111. Accessed 7 July 2026.

This is a promotional graphic representing CHIS, depicting California's outline filled with survey topic keywords alongside the CHIS logo. We used this image as our website's header banner to visually establish our project's connection to the CHIS dataset.
